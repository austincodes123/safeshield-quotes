Why a USB-C Charger?

Your laptop ships with an American charger that will not plug into South African outlets. Instead of dealing with adapters, we are having you order a 65W USB-C charger that comes with a South Africa plug built in. This plugs right into the dock and powers everything.

How It All Connects

The USB-C dock is the center of your setup. It lets you plug one single cable into your laptop and get power, your monitor, keyboard, and mouse all connected at once.

1

Charger to Dock

Plug the 65W USB-C charger into the wall. Then plug the USB-C end of the charger into the Power Delivery (PD) port on the dock.

2

Monitor to Dock

Take your HDMI cable. Plug one end into the back of the Dell monitor. Plug the other end into the HDMI port on the dock.

3

Keyboard + Mouse to Dock

The Logitech MK270 comes with a tiny USB receiver (a small dongle). Plug it into one of the USB-A ports on the dock. Both the keyboard and mouse connect wirelessly through that one receiver.

4

Dock to Laptop

The dock has a USB-C cable built in. Plug that into your laptop's USB-C port. As soon as you do this, your laptop starts charging, the monitor turns on, and your keyboard and mouse are connected. One cable does everything.

5

Power On the Monitor

Press the power button on the bottom or side of the Dell monitor. It should detect the signal from your laptop automatically.

The One-Cable Lifestyle

When you are done for the day, unplug one cable from your laptop and you are free. When you sit back down the next morning, plug that one cable back in and everything is ready to go. Monitor, keyboard, mouse, power. All from one plug.

Setting Up Your Displays

Once your monitor is connected and showing an image, set up how your two screens work together.

Display Configuration (Windows)

1. Right-click anywhere on your desktop and select Display settings.
2. You will see two rectangles labeled 1 and 2. These represent your laptop screen and your monitor. Click Identify to see which number is which.
3. Under Multiple displays, select Extend these displays. This gives you two separate screens to work with instead of a mirror.
4. Drag the rectangles to match how your screens are physically positioned. If your monitor is to the right of your laptop, drag rectangle 2 to the right. This way your mouse moves naturally between screens.
5. Click Apply to save your layout.
6. To set your main display (where your taskbar and icons appear), click the rectangle for that screen and check Make this my main display. We recommend making the big monitor your main display.

Pro Tip

Most people keep their main work (NowCerts, PL Rater, email) on the big monitor and use the laptop screen for Google Chat, reference documents, or notes. Find what works for you and stick with it.
