Your Coverage

What's Protected

Every coverage below was selected to protect you as a landlord. Here's what each one does and why it matters.

🏠

Dwelling Coverage

$580,000

This is the big one. It covers the cost to repair or rebuild your property if it's damaged by fire, storms, vandalism, or other covered events. This limit reflects the estimated cost to rebuild the entire structure.

🏗️

Other Structures

$58,000

Covers detached structures on the property like garages, sheds, or fences. Set at 10% of your dwelling limit, which is standard and usually plenty.

💰

Fair Rental Value

$58,000

If a covered loss makes your property uninhabitable, this reimburses the rental income you'd lose while repairs are underway. Critical for a 2-family rental.

⚖️

Premises Liability

$500,000

If a tenant, visitor, or delivery person gets hurt on your property and sues you, this pays for legal defense and any settlement. As a landlord, this is essential.

🩺

Medical Payments

$500 / person

Pays small medical bills if someone gets injured on your property, regardless of fault. Helps resolve minor incidents before they turn into lawsuits. $25,000 max per occurrence.

💧

Water Backup & Sump Overflow

$5,000

Covers damage caused by water that backs up through drains or sump pumps. This is one of the most common claims for older homes with basements. $250 deductible.

🔧

Ordinance or Law

$58,000

Older homes like yours (built 1890) may not meet current building codes. If you rebuild after a loss, this pays the extra cost to bring everything up to modern code requirements.

🦠

Mold Remediation

$10,000

Covers mold cleanup and remediation on the property side, plus $50,000 on the liability side if a tenant claims mold-related health issues.

🛡️

Lead & Fuel Oil Liability

$50,000

Specifically covers claims related to lead paint exposure or escaped liquid fuel. For a pre-1978 home, this is a valuable layer of protection. Also includes $10,000 for fuel-related property damage.

🔐

Additional Coverages

Included

Vandalism & malicious mischief, full water damage, property manager premises liability extension, and $500 fire department service charge are all baked in at no extra cost.
