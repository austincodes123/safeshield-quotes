Brand Identity

What do we call this thing?

New brand. Separate from Safeshield. Under Terrpain Ventures. Click any name to favorite it. These are starting points to riff on.

DESKBOUND
"Your agency. All in one place."
Where the work happens. The desk. Speaks to the physical reality of being an agent: everything lands at the desk, and now everything runs from it. Clean, grounded, no InsurTech buzzwords.
BASECAMP
"Your operation starts here."
HQ energy. The foundation. Where every expedition launches from. Implies structure and support without being corporate. Problem: 37signals owns the project management brand.
LOCKBOX
"Everything your agency runs on. Locked in."
Insurance industry resonance (lockbox payments are a real thing). Implies security, containment, everything in one vault. Dark, premium, slightly aggressive. Memorable.
PROWL
"Hunt. Quote. Close. Repeat."
Predatory energy. Agents are hunters. The platform makes them dangerous. Short, punchy, brandable, domain-friendly. Different from everything in the space.
MAINLINE
"One line. Every tool."
Direct injection. The main artery everything flows through. Implies connectivity and centrality. Has tech energy without sounding like a startup that'll fold in 18 months.
BULLPEN
"Built by the guy in the chair."
Where the closers warm up. Baseball heritage. "The bullpen" is where the work gets done before it hits the mound. Already in Austin's vocabulary (office display). Raw, authentic, team energy.
KEYSTROKE
"One platform. Zero re-keying."
Directly attacks the #1 pain point: agents re-key the same data 8 times a day across portals. "Keystroke" is the problem AND the solution. Enter it once, never again. Clever double meaning.

Positioning

Different pitch.
Same killer product.

The product doesn't change. The hook does. Here's how the story lands for each buyer.

Solo / Scratch Agent
"This is your staff."
You can't afford a CSR, a VA, and a rater subscription. You're re-keying data for 3 hours a day. This platform IS your team. AI answers questions, the rater quotes 8 carriers while you're on the phone, commissions calculate themselves. You just produce.
Small Team (3-10)
"Your best producers are doing admin all day. Stop that."
Every hour your top closer spends in a carrier portal is revenue lost. One platform. One entry. All carriers quoted. All calls logged. All commissions tracked. Your producers produce. The system handles the rest.
Established Agency
"You're paying $500+/month for a CMS built in 2009. This one thinks."
Your AMS doesn't talk to your rater. Your rater doesn't talk to your phone system. Your phone system doesn't log to client files. You're duct-taping 6 tools together. Replace all of them. One login. One bill.

Competitive Moat

Why nobody can copy this.

01

Built by a Producer

Not by a dev who's never sat in the chair. Every workflow, every field, every confirmation card was designed by someone who writes policies. That's not a tagline. That's the product spec. No InsurTech company can claim this.

02

The Ivans Moat

Carrier downloads, policy changes, endorsements, cancellations, AND commission statements flowing into your own database automatically, for free, every single day. That's infrastructure NowCerts charges $535/month to provide. This platform does it natively.

03

True Vertical Integration

AMS, rater, phone, commissions, AI. Not five vendors duct-taped together. Not "partnerships" that break when APIs change. Five native components that share one database. Build the household once, everything downstream just works.

04

AI That Understands Insurance

The brain isn't a chatbot bolted on for marketing. It's the interface layer. It reads your AMS, drafts in your voice, extracts dec pages by vision, knows your carriers, your book, your renewals. It's been trained on a live operating agency since day one.

05

Proof of Concept = Live Agency

Safeshield runs on this platform today. $2.4M book. Real clients. Real commissions. Real renewals. It's not a prototype or a demo environment. The pitch is: "We run our agency on this. You should too."
