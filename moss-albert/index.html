The Real Headlines

What you're
actually gaining.

01

Dwelling properly insured.

Your 5,500 sqft home was sitting at $1.65M of dwelling coverage with Utica. For a home that size, that was thin. Kingstone writes it at $2.79M, which is where it should be to fully rebuild if the worst happens.

02

Inland flood added.

This wasn't on your Utica policy at all. Kingstone bakes in $15,000 of inland flood coverage automatically. Small limit, but real protection for a risk you weren't covered for before.

03

House on the market? Still approved.

Most carriers run for the hills when a home is listed. Kingstone is fully aware and still binding the risk. That means we don't have to scramble for a surplus lines placement at 2x the cost.

04

Umbrella, half the price.

Same $1M limit, same protection over your home and auto. Premium drops from $573 to $297. No change in coverage or underlying requirements, just a better rate.

The Umbrella

Same protection.

Your $1M personal umbrella sits over your home liability and auto liability. Nothing about that coverage changes. The underlying limits on your Hartford and Travelers auto policies still satisfy Kingstone's requirements at 250/500/100.

The only thing that changes is the price.

One thing worth knowing.

Kingstone writes this as an HO3 policy form versus Utica's HO5. In practice, that means personal property (your contents) is covered on a named-perils basis instead of open-perils. The dwelling itself is still covered on an open-perils basis, which is what matters most.

On a home you are currently preparing to sell, this is a non-issue. The overwhelming majority of homeowner claims are dwelling-related, not contents-related, and the contents coverage itself still includes replacement cost settlement.

If you want to keep HO5 specifically, we can discuss other carrier options, but the savings and coverage upgrades on this Kingstone quote are hard to walk away from.
