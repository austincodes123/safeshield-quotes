What this means in plain English: Right now, if Cameron causes an accident and seriously injures someone, his GEICO policy maxes out at $25,000 per person. In New York, a single ER visit with surgery can easily exceed $100,000. That means Cameron would be personally responsible for everything above $25K out of his own pocket. With the NYCM policy, that same scenario is covered up to $250,000 per person and $500,000 per accident. The property damage limit jumps from $25K to $100K, which matters when you consider that even a fender bender with a newer luxury car can run $30-40K in repairs. And the SUM (Supplementary Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist) coverage goes from $25K/$50K to $250K/$500K, meaning if someone without enough insurance hits them, they're covered at the same high limits. All of this for $1,471 less per year.
