What Each Coverage Means

🏠

Dwelling: $577,000

This might look low compared to your $825,000 purchase price, but that's by design. Your purchase price includes the land, the location, and the market value. None of that burns down. The $577,000 represents what it would actually cost to rebuild the physical structure from the ground up. And here's the key: you also have 50% extended replacement cost built in, which adds another $288,500 of protection. That brings your total rebuild coverage to $865,500, which actually exceeds your purchase price. Your lender will be more than satisfied.

🏣

Other Structures: $28,850

Covers structures on your property that aren't attached to the house: a detached garage, shed, fence, etc. Automatically set at 5% of your dwelling value.

👚

Personal Property: $288,500

Your stuff. Furniture, clothes, electronics, appliances. If it's damaged or stolen, this pays to replace it. You have replacement cost coverage, which means you get the price of a brand new item, not a depreciated value.

🌈

Loss of Use: $115,400

If your home is so damaged you can't live in it while repairs happen, this pays for a hotel, meals, and extra living expenses. It keeps you on your feet during a worst-case scenario.

🛡

Personal Liability: $300,000

If someone gets hurt on your property (slips on ice, trips on stairs) and sues you, this covers their medical bills and your legal defense. $300,000 is a strong starting limit for a new homeowner.

🩹

Medical Payments: $5,000

If a guest has a minor injury at your home, this pays their medical bills quickly with no lawsuit needed. It's the "good neighbor" coverage that keeps small incidents from becoming big problems.

What About Deductibles?

Your deductible is what you pay out of pocket before insurance kicks in. You have two:

1

All Other Perils: $1,000

For most claims (fire, theft, water damage, etc.), you pay $1,000 and insurance covers the rest.

2

Hurricane: $11,540 (2%)

Hurricane deductibles in New York are percentage-based. This only applies during a named hurricane, not a regular storm or windstorm.

Included Extras

+

Extended Replacement Cost: 50%

This is the coverage that brings your total rebuild protection to $865,500. If construction costs spike after a disaster (lumber shortages, labor demand, etc.), this extra 50% cushion kicks in so you're not left short. This is a major coverage feature that many policies don't include.

+

Law & Ordinance: 10%

If you have to rebuild and new building codes require upgrades (electrical, plumbing, etc.), this covers up to 10% of your dwelling value for those code-related costs.
