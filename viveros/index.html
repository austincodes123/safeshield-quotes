Personal Umbrella

The most important dollar you'll spend on insurance.

A personal umbrella sits on top of your auto and home limits, takes over when they're exhausted, and protects your personal assets from a catastrophic claim.

The rule is simple: your umbrella limit should equal or exceed your net worth. For any household worth more than $500,000, an umbrella is no longer optional — it's the layer that prevents a single bad day from undoing years of work.

One serious at-fault accident in a vehicle like the Macan, with a passenger injured, can exhaust $500,000 in liability limits quickly. An umbrella adds $1M or more of additional protection for roughly $1.04 to $3.43 a day depending on the limit selected.
