⚑ Why These Holes Matter
⚠️
Hole #1: The $131,500 Dwelling Gap
This is the single biggest risk on the scorecard. Geico values the dwelling at $468,500 for a home being purchased at $915,000 in Eastchester. Could we quote you at that same number and save you a few bucks? Absolutely. But we would not recommend it, and here is why: rebuilding a 126-year-old frame home in Westchester County today means specialized labor, materials, and code compliance costs that far exceed national averages. At $468K, you are not insuring your home to be rebuilt. You are insuring it to be partially rebuilt, and you are covering the rest out of pocket. If a total loss occurs, you could be six figures short with no way to recover it. That is not a coverage gap. That is a financial disaster. We set you at $600,000 because that is what it actually takes to put this house back together.

Hole #5: The Hurricane Sand Trap
Geico's 5% hurricane deductible means you write a $23,425 check before a single dollar of coverage kicks in during a named storm. That is nearly the cost of a brand new car. The NYCM policy keeps the hurricane deductible at a flat $2,500, which is the same as any other claim. In the lower NY metro area, hurricanes and tropical storms are not hypothetical. Superstorm Sandy proved that. Isaias proved that. This is real money at real risk.

🏘️
The 126-Year-Old Factor: Ordinance or Law
A home built in 1899 is almost certainly not up to modern building codes. If a major loss occurs, the town of Eastchester will require updated electrical, plumbing, and HVAC during the rebuild. These code-compliance upgrades are expensive and not covered under a standard policy without Ordinance or Law endorsements. The NYCM quote includes additional limits of liability that help absorb these costs. With Geico's already-low dwelling limit, even their standard 10% Ordinance or Law provision barely moves the needle. You would be rebuilding a 2026-code house on an 1899-code budget.
🏆
Hole #11: New for Old, Not Garage Sale Prices
The NYCM policy includes Replacement Cost on contents. If your 3-year-old TV gets destroyed, the policy pays for a brand new equivalent. Geico uses Actual Cash Value, which depreciates belongings based on age. That same TV might be valued at a fraction of what it costs to replace. Over a full home's worth of furniture, electronics, clothing, and personal items, this difference adds up fast. You do not want to find out what your couch is "worth" to an adjuster using depreciation tables.
⛳
Hole #8: 5x Guest Medical Protection
Geico provides $1,000 in medical payments if a guest gets hurt on your property. One trip to the ER blows through that in minutes. The NYCM policy provides $5,000, which covers real-world medical costs and keeps small incidents from turning into lawsuits. When your buddy trips on the patio at a barbecue, you want this number to actually mean something.
💫
Hole #10: Personal Injury, a.k.a. The Caddy's Advice
Geico does not include Personal Injury coverage at all. The NYCM policy does. This protects against claims of libel, slander, defamation, or invasion of privacy. In 2026, one social media post that goes sideways can trigger a lawsuit. This is cheap protection that the other policy simply does not offer. It is like playing without a caddy and hoping you read the green right on your own.
